About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Assistant at the University of Glasgow, where I work on AI-driven epidemic modelling, graph-based inference, and computational approaches to infectious disease prediction.

My research integrates mechanistic epidemiological models, graph neural networks, and genomic data to study disease transmission in complex and data-limited settings. I completed my PhD at the Kirby Institute at UNSW, where I developed mechanistic models for COVID-19 vaccination strategy evaluation, agent-based models for synthetic contact matrix generation in aged care settings, and phylogenetic approaches to investigate clustering in human avian influenza cases.

Selected Publications

  • A Probabilistic Framework for Imputing Genetic Distances in Spatiotemporal Pathogen Models
    Haley Stone, Jing Du, Hao Xue, Matthew Scotch, David Heslop, Andreas Züfle, C. Raina MacIntyre, Flora Salim
    ACM SIGSPATIAL International Conference on Advances in Geographic Information Systems, 2025
    Paper Code Abstract
    Pathogen genome data offers valuable structure for spatial models, but its utility is limited by incomplete sequencing coverage. We propose a probabilistic framework for inferring genetic distances between unsequenced cases and known sequences using time-aware evolutionary distance modelling.
  • Genomic-Informed Heterogeneous Graph Learning for Spatiotemporal Avian Influenza Outbreak Forecasting
    Jing Du, Haley Stone, Yang Yang, Ashna Desai, Hao Xue, Andreas Züfle, C. Raina MacIntyre, Flora Salim
    Web4Good Workshop, 2026
    Paper Code Abstract
    We propose BLUE, a genomic-aware heterogeneous graph framework integrating ecological, spatial, and genetic information to improve avian influenza outbreak forecasting in sparse surveillance settings.
  • From Ecological Connectivity to Outbreak Risk: A Heterogeneous Graph Network for Epidemiological Reasoning under Sparse Spatiotemporal Data
